We're going to make Delaware the most accessible state in the country.

From parks to beaches, from rivers to oceans, Delaware is full of all kinds of natural beauty. While thousands of families visit each of these locations each year, some people simply don’t have access to the resources or transportation to make those trips a possibility. There are plenty of reasons for that – from unwieldy bus routes to issues for people with disabilities. Over the next few months the Open Data Challenge is going to work to break down some of those barriers.

 

How We’ll Do It

Breaking down those barriers is a process that requires help from everyone. We call that process the Open Data Challenge. At the end of the Open Data Challenge, we’ll be left with proof-of-concepts that can be implemented in the community. There are a few key events that make up the Open Data Challenge: workshops, an ideation session, and a Data Jam. Each of these events is free to participate in and open to the public.

 

Workshops

Level up your skills, from data visualization to storytelling, in one of our workshops.

Ideation Session

Work with experts and community members to build prototype solutions.

Data Jam

Form teams and compete for prizes in this month-long hackathon.

Why You Should Participate

We can think of a whole bunch of reasons that you might want to consider. Here’s a few really good ones.

 

Level Up Your Skills

When you participate in the Open Data Challenge, you’ll have the chance to sharpen your skills or learn new ones – whether those are soft skills or technical!

 

Help Your Community

You’ll be directly helping to make a difference in your Delaware community. Throughout the Open Data Challenge, you’ll be talking directly with community stakeholders and your ideas could end up being implemented state-wide.

 

Win Cash Prizes

There are up to $35,000 in cash prizes available based upon the work that teams complete throughout the Open Data Challenge.
